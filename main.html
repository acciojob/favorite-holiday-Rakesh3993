Manali, Himachal Pradesh


Tucked away in the majestic Himalayas, Manali is one of India's most loved hill stations. With its snowy peaks, serene valleys, and the roaring Beas River cutting through, Manali is a perfect blend of adventure and tranquility. It's ideal for backpackers, honeymooners, nature lovers, and thrill-seekers alike. Whether you're paragliding over Solang Valley, trekking through pine forests, or just sipping chai by a riverside cafe—Manali is a vibe.

  1. Solang Valley: A go-to spot for snow sports in winter and paragliding, zorbing, and quad biking in summer. The views? Unreal.
  2. Rohtang Pass: One of the highest mountain passes accessible by road. Snow lovers, this is your paradise! (Usually open from May to November.)
  3. Old Manali: Chill cafes, boho vibes, wooden homes, and apple orchards—Old Manali feels like a different world. Great place to unwind.
  4. Hidimba Devi Temple: A unique temple surrounded by deodar forests, built in 1553, dedicated to Hidimba from the Mahabharata.
  5. Jogini Waterfall: A scenic short trek from Vashisht village takes you to this peaceful waterfall with a stunning view of the valley.
  • Siddu: A traditional Himachali stuffed bread made with wheat flour, typically filled with walnut or poppy seed paste, and steamed.
  • Trout Fish: Freshwater trout grilled or fried—often caught locally from the Beas River. A delicacy in the region.
  • Madra: A curd-based curry made with chickpeas or kidney beans, cooked with a variety of spices—rich and aromatic.
  • Chha Gosht: Mutton slow-cooked in a spicy, tangy yogurt gravy. Best had with rice or Himachali roti..
  • Tudkiya Bhath: A Himachali take on pulao—spiced rice cooked with lentils, potatoes, and yogurt. Comforting and filling.
